World Wide Fund (WWF)

World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) is an international non-governmental organization that deals with issues of conservation, research and environmental restoration, formerly the World Wildlife Fund and is still the official name in Canada and the United States. WWF is the largest independent conservation organization in the world with more than 5 million supporters worldwide who work in more than 100 countries, supporting around 1,300 conservation and environmental projects. WWF is a foundation which in 2010 received 57% of its funding from individuals and inheritance, 17% from international sources (such as the World Bank, DFID, USAID) and 11% from various companies. This group has mission "to prevent and reverse the destruction of our environment". At present, most of their work is focused on the conservation of three biomes which contain most of the world's biodiversity, namely forests, freshwater ecosystems, and oceans and beaches. In addition, WWF also addresses the problem of endangered species, pollution and climate change.
